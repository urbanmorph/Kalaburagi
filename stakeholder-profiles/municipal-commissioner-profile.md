# Municipal Commissioner - Kalaburagi City Corporation

## Role Overview

The Municipal Commissioner is the chief executive officer and administrative head of the Kalaburagi City Corporation (Mahanagara Palike), responsible for the day-to-day administration and implementation of urban development policies.

## Appointment & Authority

- **Appointed by:** State Government of Karnataka
- **Cadre:** Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer
- **Reporting:** Reports to Commissioner and Director of Municipal Administration (CDMA), Karnataka
- **Executive Authority:** Full administrative control over municipal operations and staff

## Key Functions & Responsibilities

### 1. Urban Planning & Development
- Oversee comprehensive urban planning for Kalaburagi city
- Implement Master Plan and zoning regulations
- Coordinate with Kalaburagi Urban Development Authority (KUDA)
- Approve building plans and development projects
- Manage urban expansion and infrastructure development

### 2. Infrastructure Management

  - Coordinate with PWD on major roads
  - Street lighting and traffic management

- **Water Supply:**
  - Ensure 24x7 water supply to urban wards
  - Manage water treatment plants and distribution
  - Address water quality and availability issues
  - Coordinate with Water Resources Department

- **Sanitation & Solid Waste:**
  - Manage solid waste collection and disposal
  - Implement Swachh Bharat Mission
  - Drainage and sewerage system maintenance
  - Public toilet facilities

### 3. Budget & Financial Management
- Prepare annual budget for municipal corporation
- Ensure efficient revenue collection (property tax, user charges)
- Monitor expenditure and budget utilization
- Implement financial reforms and audit compliance
- Manage grants from state and central government

### 4. Housing & Slum Development
- Implement Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY-Urban)
- Slum rehabilitation and upgradation
- Affordable housing projects
- Coordinate with Karnataka Housing Board
- Worker housing for industrial projects (PM MITRA, garment units)

### 5. Public Health & Sanitation
- Disease prevention and health surveillance
- Manage urban health centers and dispensaries
- Vector control and sanitation drives
- Birth and death registration
- COVID-19 and pandemic management

### 6. Social Welfare & Community Development
- Programs for weaker sections and marginalized communities
- Services for elderly, disabled, and vulnerable groups
- Women and child development initiatives
- Poverty alleviation programs
- Community participation and grievance redressal

### 7. Environmental Management
- Urban forestry and green space development
- Air quality monitoring and pollution control
- Climate resilience and adaptation measures
- Waste management and recycling
- Environmental clearances for projects

### 8. Scheme Implementation
- Central schemes: AMRUT, Smart Cities, Swachh Bharat Mission
- State schemes: Nagarothana, KUWSDB projects
- Industrial support infrastructure for PM MITRA and new industries
- Skill development and employment generation

### 9. Administrative Leadership
- Supervise municipal staff (all departments)
- Implement corporation decisions and resolutions
- Coordinate with elected representatives (Mayor, Corporators)
- Inter-departmental coordination
- Performance monitoring and accountability

## Key Performance Indicators for Kalaburagi

### Current Urban Profile (2024-25)
- **City Population:** ~5.5 lakh (2024 estimate, up from 5.43 lakh in 2011)
- **Municipal Area:** 62.5 sq km
- **Number of Wards:** 55 wards
- **Road Network:** 753 km
- **Water Supply:** Partial coverage, gaps in 24x7 supply
- **Sanitation:** Basic infrastructure, needs expansion

### Priority Development Areas (2026-2034 Framework)

#### 1. Industrial Worker Housing
- **Challenge:** PM MITRA textile park will bring 26,000 garment workers by 2034
- **Need:** 8,000 worker hostel beds
- **Action:** Coordinate with KHB and private developers for worker housing near industrial areas

#### 2. Urban Infrastructure Upgrades
- **Roads:** Upgrade approach roads to KIADB industrial areas (Sedam, Kalaburagi Growth Centre)
- **Water:** Ensure 10 MLD additional supply for industrial expansion
- **Sanitation:** Drainage upgrades for industrial zones
- **Power:** Coordinate with GESCOM on 24x7 supply

#### 3. Slum Development
- **Current:** Significant slum population requiring basic services
- **Target:** Complete slum upgradation under PMAY-Urban by 2029
- **Focus:** Water, sanitation, electricity, pucca housing

#### 4. Environmental Management
- **Solid Waste:** Improve collection and scientific disposal
- **Air Quality:** Monitor pollution from industries and traffic
- **Green Cover:** Increase urban green spaces (target: 10% green cover)

#### 5. Service Delivery
- **Property Tax:** Improve collection efficiency (target: 85% collection rate)
- **Citizen Services:** Digitize services and reduce turnaround time
- **Grievance Redressal:** 80% grievances resolved within 15 days

## Coordination Requirements

### With District Administration
- **District Collector:** Overall development coordination, land acquisition
- **SP:** Law and order, especially during festivals and events
- **Revenue Department:** Land records, mutations, conversions

### With State Departments
- **CDMA:** Policy guidelines, funding approvals, audit compliance
- **PWD:** Major roads, bridges, state highways
- **KUDA:** Urban planning, master plan, development permissions
- **GESCOM:** Power supply and distribution
- **Water Resources:** Bulk water supply, reservoirs

### With Industrial Development
- **KIADB:** Industrial area infrastructure coordination
- **Industries Department:** Support for MSME, new industries
- **Skill Development:** Training for urban unemployed youth

### With Central Schemes
- **AMRUT:** Water supply and sewerage projects
- **Smart Cities Mission:** Urban innovation projects
- **Swachh Bharat Mission:** Sanitation and solid waste management
- **PMAY-Urban:** Affordable housing

## Challenges Specific to Kalaburagi

1. **Rapid Industrialization:** Need to scale urban services for 156,700 new jobs by 2034
2. **Water Scarcity:** Drought-prone region, ensuring adequate water supply critical
3. **Migration Management:** In-migration for industrial jobs will strain infrastructure
4. **Informal Settlements:** Growing slums requiring planned intervention
5. **Resource Constraints:** Limited municipal revenue base for ambitious development agenda
6. **Coordination Complexity:** Multiple parallel development projects require tight coordination

## Success Metrics (2026-2034)

- **Water Supply:** 24x7 supply to all urban wards by 2029
- **Sanitation:** 100% household toilet coverage by 2027
- **Housing:** 15,000 PMAY houses sanctioned and 80% completed by 2030
- **Roads:** 90% roads in good condition (no potholes, proper drainage)
- **Solid Waste:** 100% door-to-door collection and 80% scientific disposal by 2028
- **Revenue:** Property tax collection efficiency above 85% by 2029
- **Citizen Satisfaction:** 70%+ satisfaction in service delivery surveys
