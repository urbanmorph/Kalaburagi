## WHY SO MINIMAL?

### Likely Reasons for Limited Inclusion:

1. **Framework Focus:** The implementation framework prioritizes **rural development** (water, agriculture, processing) over urban transport
   - 88% rural population
   - Agriculture as primary constraint
   - Urban transport is Phase 3 (not immediate priority)

2. **Budget Allocation:** 
   - Urban transport: <₹100 cr mentioned (0.6% of total ₹17,700 cr)
   - Water security: ₹2,200 cr (12.4%)
   - Agro-processing: ₹2,400 cr (13.6%)
   - MSME: ₹2,500 cr (14.1%)

3. **DULT Document May Be Dated:**
   - CTTP 2011 is 15 years old
   - Kalaburagi has changed (airport operational 2019)
   - May need updated CTTP rather than implementing 2011 plan

4. **Urban vs Rural Balance:**
   - Only 33.8% urban population
   - Focus on solving rural poverty/migration first
   - Urban transport becomes relevant in Phase 3 (2032-2034)

## CURRENT STATUS ASSESSMENT

### Is Current Inclusion Adequate? 

**YES, for now** - Here's why:

1. **Strategic Prioritization:**
   - Kalaburagi's rank 30/30 is due to rural poverty, not urban congestion
   - 88% rainfed agriculture is the binding constraint
   - Water security must precede urban transport

2. **Phased Approach:**
   - Phase 1-2 (Years 1-6): Fix rural fundamentals
   - Phase 3 (Years 7-8): Scale urban services
   - Current mention establishes intent, details come later

3. **Resource Constraints:**
   - PMU has 5 core staff + 11 data officers
   - Cannot execute 100 schemes simultaneously
   - Focus on high-impact, low-complexity interventions first

4. **Political Economy:**
   - 66% rural population votes
   - Visible water/jobs wins elections
   - Urban transport benefits smaller constituency

### When to Expand DULT Content?

**Trigger Points:**

1. **Year 3 (2028):** If urban population crosses 40%
2. **Year 4 (2029):** When Phase 2 stabilizes, capacity available
3. **Year 5 (2030):** If traffic congestion becomes political issue
4. **Year 6 (2031):** Mandatory for Phase 3 planning

**Indicators to Watch:**
- Urban population growth rate
- Private vehicle registrations
- Peak hour congestion levels
- Public transport ridership decline
- Citizen complaints on traffic
